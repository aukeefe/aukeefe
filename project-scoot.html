Scoot

Scoot Airline is a low-cost long-haul airline owned by Singapore Airlines. It launched flights in 2012 on medium and long-haul routes from Singapore, predominantly to Australia and China.

Task

Scoot Airline wanted to update and revamp their current cart recovery email templates with their latest branding guidelines.

I am also assigned to create an On-Site marketing modal box on their flight payment checkout page. The objective of the modal box is to encourage customers to continue with their booking before they leave or close the web browser. The design needs to include a message that conveys a sense of urgency. It needs to have an option for the users to email a copy of their flight selection to their email address so that they can continue booking at a later stage.

Idea

My first approach is to read through the new brand guidelines and then examine client's current email template.

For this project, I used Balsamiq to create a rough design layout for the new email template. I find that Balsamiq is a great wireframing tool to create fast low-fidelity wireframes.

First, new logo and color theme need to be updated. Next, to improve the design of their email template, I want to include a better image and copy in the header to convey a sense of excitement and urgency. I also want to improve their cart layout so that it's easier to read. Lastly, I want to remove all the unnecessary design elements in the footer.

On-Site marketing wireframe

For the On-Site marketing modal box design, I wanted to have the modal box pop up from the bottom of the web page. The modal box needs to draw a user's attention before he or she closes the web browser.

I need to include an email input field and CTA button in the design so that users can either continue with their booking or send a copy of their flight selection to their email inbox. I also want to have an image that will animate in from the left.

Build

For my build process, I like to hand-code the template from scratch in either Adobe Dreamweaver or Sublime Text. Usually, I use a basic template that I have created, and I use Snippets to speed up my design workflow.

To ensure the images look sharp on mobile devices, I export all the images from Adobe Photoshop at 2x or 3x. For standardization, all image assets are named ending with @2x or @3x. I always include alt text for all the images in the email template for web accessibility.

I like to use the Developer Tools in Google Chrome while I'm coding the template. This method allows me to troubleshoot any errors at an early stage before email testing in Litmus.

On-Site marketing animation

While coding the build for the On-Site marketing modal box design, I prefer to test it and preview the design in Google Chrome. I like to make sure the CSS animations is working the way I wanted.

For this design, the modal box will slide up when the user moves the mouse cursor out of the viewport, the visible area of the webpage within the browser window.

The challenging part was to make the luggage image animate the way I wanted. I want the luggage image to animate as if something is alive inside. To achieve this effect, I use the CSS3 transform property values - rotate, skew, scale, translateX, and translateY.

Testing

The most challenging part of Email Design is making sure the template works in all of the popular email clients. Litmus is a great tool to test and troubleshoot an email template.

The most common errors usually happen in Outlook especially Outlook version 2007, 2010 & 2013. For example, adding an inline CSS - mso-line-height-rule:exactly; for the paragraph of text solve the extra line height issue.

The email template works perfectly on all desktop and mobile devices. I received great feedback from the client that they love the new email template and the On-Site marketing design. The client also sees an increased rate of cart recovery.
